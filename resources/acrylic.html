Acrylic art process

A brief showcase of our professional nail artist on Acrylic art

Step 1: Prepare the nail

Nail preparation is a crucial step in the nail art process. It not only contributes to the overall appearance of the nails but also plays a significant role in the longevity and durability of the nail enhancements. Taking the time to properly prepare the nails sets the stage for a successful and professional-looking acrylic nail art application.

Step 2: Cleanse the nail

Cleaning the nails ensures that they are free from any natural oils, dirt, or residue. This clean surface provides a better foundation for the adhesion of the acrylic material, allowing it to bond more securely to the natural nails.

Step 3: Remove the natural shine

It's important to note that excessive buffing or filing can weaken the natural nails, so it should be done carefully and in moderation. Additionally, after the acrylic application and shaping, a topcoat or sealant is usually applied to restore shine and protect the acrylic nails. Proper nail preparation is a key step in the overall process, ensuring the best results for both the appearance and durability of the acrylic nail art.

Step 4: Tapered cuticle bit

It's important to note that using these tools requires skill and experience, as improper use can potentially damage the natural nails or cuticles. Nail technicians should be trained in the correct techniques and safety practices when using tapered cuticle bits or any other rotary tools.

Step 5: Apply nail tip

Applying a nail tip is a common technique in the process of creating artificial nails, especially when working with techniques like acrylic or gel extensions.

Step 6: Finish with Norish oil

Finishing with nourishing oil is a wonderful way to complete the nail care process, leaving your nails and cuticles looking and feeling their best. This step is especially beneficial after any procedures that may have involved the use of acetone or other products that could potentially dry out the nails and surrounding skin.

Step 7: Shape the nail

Shaping the nails is an important step in achieving a polished and professional look. Whether you're working with natural nails or artificial enhancements like acrylic or gel

Step 8: Remove Ledge

Always work with caution and patience to avoid over-filing or damaging the artificial nails. If you're unsure or uncomfortable doing this yourself, consider seeking the assistance of a professional nail technician who can address the issue effectively.

Step 9: Apply Dehydrator

Dehydrator is a solution used in the nail care and nail enhancement process to remove excess oils and moisture from the natural nails. It helps improve adhesion when applying artificial materials like acrylic or gel.

Step 10: Apply Primer

Primer is a crucial step in the application of acrylic or gel nail enhancements. It helps improve adhesion by preparing the natural nail surface.

Step 11: Apply clear Acrylic

Applying clear acrylic is a fundamental step in creating artificial nail extensions.

Step 12: Wet bead

n acrylic nail application, the term "wet bead" refers to a small, wet and pliable amount of acrylic mixture (acrylic powder and liquid monomer) that is picked up by the acrylic brush for sculpting and shaping on the nails. Creating a wet bead is an essential skill for nail technicians working with acrylics

Step 13: Dry bead

In acrylic nail application, a "dry bead" refers to a small, dry, and firm amount of acrylic mixture (acrylic powder and liquid monomer) that is picked up by the acrylic brush for sculpting and shaping on the nails. Creating a dry bead is an essential skill for nail technicians working with acrylics.

Step 14: Bead method

In acrylic nail application, the "bead method" refers to the technique of picking up a specific amount of acrylic mixture (acrylic powder and liquid monomer) with an acrylic brush to sculpt and shape artificial nails. This method involves creating either a "wet bead" or a "dry bead," as mentioned

Step 15: Apply color

Applying color to acrylic nails involves adding pigmented acrylic powder to create a decorative and personalized look

Step 16: Apex

In the context of acrylic nails and nail enhancements, the term "apex" refers to the highest point or arch in the structure of the nail extension. The apex is a critical aspect of creating a well-balanced and durable acrylic nail

Step 1: Clean-off nail

Cleaning off the nail surface is an essential step in the process of applying and maintaining artificial nails, especially when working with acrylic or gel materials.

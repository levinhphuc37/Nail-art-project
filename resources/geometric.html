Buffing your nails is a simple process that can help improve their appearance by smoothing the surface and enhancing shine.

Step 2: Dehydrate your nail

Cleaning the nails ensures that they are free from any natural oils, dirt, or residue. This clean surface provides a better foundation for the adhesion , allowing it to bond more securely to the natural nails.

Step 3: Prime your nail

"Priming" the nails prepares them for nail polish or other nail enhancements.

Step 4: Cover with base coat cure in lamp

Base coat application and maintenance are intended to protect your natural nails.

Step 5: Distribute polygeo

This is the step to evenly distribute the shapes on the nail.

Step 6: Cure in lamp

Lamp cure" is the process of using UV or LED lamps to cure (harden or set) gel nail polish and improve gel nails.

Step 7: Remove sticky layer

In the context of gel nail polish or gel nail enhancements, a "sticky layer" often refers to the residual tacky or sticky residue that remains on the nails after curing the final layer (usually the top coat) under a UV or LED lamp. This sticky layer is also known as the "inhibition layer" or "uncured layer.

Primer is a crucial step in the application of acrylic or gel nail enhancements. It helps improve adhesion by preparing the natural nail surface.
